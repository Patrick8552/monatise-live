# Monatise Positioning

## Core Definition

Monatise is a liquidity harvesting framework and protocol.

It is designed primarily for grid-based trading, repeated liquidity placement, inventory-aware rebalancing, and spread or volatility capture. It should not be positioned as a trend-following system built around one-shot directional entries.

## Positioning Statement

Monatise is a liquidity harvesting protocol for grid-based trading strategies, designed to capture recurring market inefficiencies instead of betting on single directional moves.

## What Monatise Is

- A framework for liquidity harvesting.
- A protocol for repeated market participation.
- A grid-oriented trading system.
- A structure for placing, managing, and rebalancing liquidity across price ranges.
- A mechanism for capturing spread, volatility, and mean-reversion opportunities.
- A system that can operate through repeated small harvest cycles rather than rare large directional calls.

## What Monatise Is Not

- Not primarily a trend-following bot.
- Not a one-shot signal entry system.
- Not centered on predicting a single breakout or directional move.
- Not only an alerting layer.
- Not just a generic trading dashboard.

## Strategic Thesis

Markets often move through noisy, repeated oscillations rather than clean sustained trends. Monatise treats that movement as harvestable liquidity.

Instead of asking, "Where will price go next in one big move?", Monatise asks, "Where can liquidity be placed so repeated market movement generates harvestable flow?"

## Trading Style

Monatise should be framed around:

- Grid trading.
- Liquidity placement.
- Range participation.
- Spread capture.
- Volatility harvesting.
- Inventory balancing.
- Adaptive grid spacing.
- Risk-managed rebalancing.

The system may still observe trend, momentum, and market structure, but those signals should support liquidity deployment decisions rather than define the product as a trend-entry engine.
