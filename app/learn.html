Start here

Grid trading is a rule-based way to quote around price, not a promise that price will behave.

Monatise uses grids to place small buy and sell levels around a market mark. The edge comes from disciplined spacing, inventory control, fees, and risk limits. The danger comes from trends, leverage, over-sizing, and assuming every bounce will return.

Desk rule

Learn in paper. Trade tiny live.

  • Know the grid range before starting
  • Respect fees and funding costs
  • Stop when the market leaves the range
  • Never scale before reviewing fills

Grid Basics

Plain English
01 What the grid does

It places buy levels below the mark and sell levels above the mark so volatility can create repeated small exits.

02 When it works best

Range-bound, liquid markets are friendlier. Strong one-way trends can trap inventory or exhaust quote balance.

03 What to measure

Watch open notional, inventory ratio, order age, fill sync, drawdown, and whether the grid range still matches the market.

04 How Monatise helps

The desk separates strategy preview from live exchange orders, caps order size, and keeps start/stop under operator control.

Learning Path

From theory to desk
  1. Market mechanics Understand order types, liquidity, spread, fees, slippage, and why maker orders can still lose money.
  2. Grid construction Choose range, spacing, number of levels, order clip, inventory limits, and stop conditions.
  3. Backtesting and paper trading Run samples, compare fee-adjusted results, and test how the grid behaves when the market trends away.
  4. Live operations Start with small size, monitor exchange order count, reconcile fills, and stop when the thesis is invalid.

What Usually Goes Wrong

Risk review
  • The market trends through the lower grid and leaves the desk heavy long.
  • Fees and funding consume the small gains between levels.
  • The operator raises size after a quiet period and gets hit by volatility expansion.
  • The bot keeps quoting after the original range thesis is gone.
