About Monatise

Liquidity harvesting with discipline before prediction.

Mission

Harvest recurring market movement through structured liquidity placement.

Monatise exists to help traders capture spread, volatility, and range movement through grid-based participation, inventory-aware rebalancing, and disciplined risk control instead of reckless one-shot directional bets.

Vision

Build a disciplined liquidity-harvesting protocol for patient market participation.

The vision is a framework where traders place liquidity intelligently, protect capital first, manage inventory honestly, and turn market noise into repeatable opportunity without prediction addiction.

Operating thesis

Monatise does not ask, “Where will price go next in one big move?” It asks, “Where can liquidity be placed so repeated market movement generates harvestable flow while risk stays defined?”

Framework Grid-based liquidity placement, volatility capture, and range participation.
Risk doctrine Count the cost first: entry, invalidation, position size, target, and session condition.
Execution Use small lot size when stops widen; preserve account risk instead of forcing size.
Identity Stewardship before profit. Alignment before execution.
Grid discipline guide

Use grids as measured liquidity placement, not permission to overtrade.

A Monatise grid should be opened only after risk, invalidation, lot size, and session context are clear. The grid is a structured way to participate in repeated market movement; it is not a license to keep adding because price moved against the first level.

Count cost first Define total account risk before the first grid level. If the full grid loss is not acceptable, reduce size or do not trade.
Small lots Use smaller lot size when the stop is wide, volatility is high, or the grid needs more room to survive normal noise.
Stop math Stops are built from entry, structure invalidation, ATR, and the selected crypto asset risk band before position sizing.
Trend grid Long grids place pullback entries with stops below and targets above; short grids wait for rejection entries with stops above and targets below.
No revenge adds Do not add levels to recover a bad entry. Add only where the planned grid, structure, and risk budget already allowed it.
Stop adding Stop the grid when invalidation breaks, news changes the market state, ATR expands beyond plan, or exposure reaches the cap.
Take harvests Take partial wins as liquidity returns. Do not turn a completed harvest into a new gamble because the screen still feels active.
Peace test If the grid makes you anxious, rushed, or tempted to increase size emotionally, the correct action is pause.
